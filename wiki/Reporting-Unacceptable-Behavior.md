## 🎯 Overview

This portfolio project is committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for all contributors, users, and community members. We take reports of unacceptable behavior seriously and handle them with care, fairness, and confidentiality.

This page outlines the process for reporting incidents and explains how each report is reviewed and acted upon. All community members are expected to uphold the standards described here.

---

## ⚠️ What Constitutes Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable within this project's community:

### 🚫 Harassment & Discrimination
- **Personal attacks** - Insulting, demeaning, or derogatory comments directed at individuals
- **Discriminatory language** - Remarks related to gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, race, ethnicity, religion, disability, age, or national origin
- **Intimidation or threats** - Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or threatening behavior (online or in person)

### 🚫 Inappropriate Content
- **Sexual content** - Unwelcome sexual attention, sexually explicit comments, images, or advances
- **Violent content** - Gratuitous descriptions or images of violence
- **Hate speech** - Content that promotes hatred against any group or individual

### 🚫 Disruptive Conduct
- **Trolling** - Deliberately inflammatory, provocative, or disruptive contributions
- **Spam** - Repeated, unsolicited, or irrelevant messages
- **Doxxing** - Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- **Sustained disruption** - Continuing to disrupt discussions, issues, or pull requests after being asked to stop

### 🚫 Bad Faith Participation
- **Misrepresentation** - Impersonating other community members or project maintainers
- **Sabotage** - Deliberately submitting harmful code, breaking changes, or misleading information

> **Note:** This list is not exhaustive. The project maintainers reserve the right to take action on any behavior deemed harmful to the community, even if not explicitly listed above.

## 📝 How to Submit a Report

If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior, please report it as soon as possible. All reports are taken seriously.

### Reporting Methods

#### Option 1: GitHub Issues (Public)
For non-sensitive incidents that can be discussed openly:
1. Navigate to the [Issues page](https://github.com/Victor-Muthomi/portfolio/issues)
2. Click **New Issue**
3. Select the appropriate issue template
4. Provide a clear description of the incident

#### Option 2: Direct Contact (Private)
For sensitive or personal incidents that require confidential handling:
1. Contact the project maintainer directly via GitHub: [@Victor-Muthomi](https://github.com/Victor-Muthomi)
2. Use the GitHub profile contact options available

### What to Include in Your Report

To help us investigate effectively, please provide as much of the following as possible:

| Information | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Your contact details** | So we can follow up with you (optional if you wish to remain anonymous) |
| **Names/usernames involved** | The person(s) whose behavior you are reporting |
| **Date and time** | When the incident occurred |
| **Location** | Where it occurred (e.g., specific issue number, pull request, comment link) |
| **Description** | A clear account of what happened |
| **Evidence** | Screenshots, links, or other supporting documentation |
| **Prior incidents** | Any related previous incidents you are aware of |

> **Anonymous reports** are accepted but may limit our ability to investigate or follow up. We will still take action on anonymous reports where sufficient information is provided.

## 🔍 What Happens After You Report

Once a report is received, the following process is followed:

### Step 1: Acknowledgment
- You will receive an acknowledgment that your report has been received, typically within **48 hours**
- The report is assigned to a maintainer for review

### Step 2: Initial Review
- The assigned maintainer reviews the report and any supporting evidence
- If additional information is needed, you will be contacted
- The reported party is **not notified** at this stage

### Step 3: Investigation
- The maintainer assesses the incident against community guidelines
- All parties involved may be contacted separately and confidentially
- Evidence is reviewed objectively and impartially

### Step 4: Decision
- The maintainer determines whether a violation occurred and what action is appropriate
- The decision is documented internally for transparency and consistency

### Step 5: Action & Communication
- Appropriate enforcement action is taken (see [Enforcement Actions](#enforcement-actions))
- The reporter is notified of the outcome (subject to confidentiality constraints)
- The reported party is informed of the decision and any consequences

### Step 6: Follow-Up
- The situation is monitored to ensure the behavior does not recur
- The reporter may be contacted to confirm the issue has been resolved

## ⏱️ Response Timeline

| Stage | Target Timeline |
|-------|----------------|
| **Acknowledgment of report** | Within 48 hours |
| **Initial review complete** | Within 5 business days |
| **Investigation complete** | Within 14 calendar days |
| **Decision communicated** | Within 3 business days of investigation completion |
| **Appeal decision** | Within 14 calendar days of appeal submission |

> **Complex cases** may require additional time. If an investigation is expected to exceed these timelines, the reporter will be informed of the delay and given an updated estimate.

## ⚖️ Enforcement Actions

Depending on the severity and nature of the violation, one or more of the following actions may be taken:

### 🟡 Warning
**Used for:** Minor, first-time violations or borderline behavior

- A private written warning is issued to the individual
- The specific violation is explained clearly
- A request is made to cease the behavior immediately
- No further action is taken unless the behavior continues

### 🟠 Temporary Suspension
**Used for:** Repeated violations, more serious single incidents, or failure to comply with a prior warning

- Temporary restriction from contributing to the project
- This may include inability to comment on issues, submit pull requests, or participate in discussions
- Duration is determined based on severity (typically 1–30 days)
- Conditions for reinstatement are communicated clearly

### 🔴 Permanent Ban
**Used for:** Severe violations, credible threats, patterns of harassment, or repeated violations after suspension

- Permanent removal of the individual from all project spaces
- Blocking from the repository, issues, and discussions
- This action is irreversible except through the formal appeals process

### 🛑 Emergency Action
**Used for:** Immediate threats to community safety or severe ongoing harassment

- Immediate removal pending full investigation
- Applied when waiting for the full process would cause harm
- Full investigation still proceeds; the emergency action may be revised based on findings

> **Important:** Enforcement actions are applied progressively based on context, history, and severity. The same violation may warrant different responses depending on the overall pattern of behavior.

## 🔒 Confidentiality Policy

We are committed to protecting the privacy of all parties involved in a report.

### What We Keep Confidential
- The identity of the reporter (unless you give explicit permission to disclose)
- Details of the report that could identify the reporter
- Private communications shared during the investigation

### What We May Disclose
- General outcomes of enforcement actions (e.g., that a warning was issued) to confirm that the report was acted upon
- Information required to prevent ongoing harm to the community
- Summary information in public transparency reports (aggregated and anonymized)

### Data Retention
- Reports and investigation records are retained for **2 years** to identify patterns and inform future decisions
- Data is stored securely with access restricted to project maintainers

> **Note:** We cannot guarantee complete anonymity in all cases. If you have serious concerns about confidentiality, please contact us before submitting a report to discuss your options.

## 🔄 Appeals Process

If you believe an enforcement decision was made in error or was unfair, you may submit an appeal.

### Who Can Appeal
- **Reporters** who believe the outcome did not adequately address the behavior
- **Reported parties** who believe the decision was incorrect or disproportionate

### How to Submit an Appeal
1. Contact the project maintainer: [@Victor-Muthomi](https://github.com/Victor-Muthomi)
2. Include the following in your appeal:
   - Reference to the original incident
   - Clear explanation of why you believe the decision was incorrect
   - Any new evidence or information not previously considered

### Appeal Review Process
- Appeals are reviewed by the project maintainer (or a designated reviewer if there is a conflict of interest)
- The reviewer will examine the original decision, the appeals submission, and any new evidence
- You will receive a decision within **14 calendar days** of submitting your appeal

### Appeal Outcomes
| Outcome | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Upheld** | The original decision stands; no changes are made |
| **Modified** | The enforcement action is adjusted (increased or decreased) |
| **Reversed** | The original decision is overturned and any restrictions are lifted |

> **Note:** Appeal decisions are **final**. Repeated appeals on the same incident without new evidence will not be considered.
